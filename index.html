⚡ Lesson 1: The Golden Rule of DC Electricity

Volts ÷ Ohms = Amps

( A = V / R )

VOLT
The Engine (Push)
OHM
The Squeeze (Restriction)
AMP
The Result (Flow)

Lesson Wrap-Up

Before you test your knowledge, let's review:

Meet the three characters that run every DC circuit — and they each have one job.

⚡ Volt is the push. Volt is what gets things moving. No Volt, nothing happens.

🛑 Ohm is the squeeze. Ohm pushes back against Volt. More Ohm means less gets through.

🌊 Amp is the result. Amp is what you actually get after Volt fights through Ohm. Amp doesn't decide anything — Amp is what the circuit produces.

This is why the rule is: Volts ÷ Ohms = Amps

Amp is always the answer. You can control Volt. You can control Ohm. Amp just does the math.
